Research Projects

Rubikon: Enabling Intelligent Tutoring of 3D Physical Tasks through User Modeling and On-Demand Augmented Reality Learning
Muzhe Wu*, Haocheng Ren*, Gregory Croisdale, Anhong Guo, Xu Wang
Abstract Paper Poster
Existing video and AR tutorials for 3D physical tasks do not actively engage learners or provide adaptive feedback. Moreover, providing repeated practices for physical task learning is strenuous due to the required manual configuration of physical objects to create learning opportunities. We identify key components for 3D physical task learning that builds upon the theories of intelligent tutoring systems, with the capability to render on-demand 3D tasks through AR, and offer real-time adaptive feedback to learners. We instantiate the key components through developing Rubikon, a tutoring system for solving Rubik's Cube. We highlight three unique features of Rubikon: 1) presenting extended views of 3D objects otherwise hidden from the user; 2) generating new problems for physical tasks on-demand through AR rendering; and 3) capturing user behavior and object status through computer vision for model tracing and providing immediate feedback. We compared Rubikon with two baselines through a between-subjects experiment with 36 participants, and demonstrated the effectiveness of Rubikon in yielding higher learning gains, due to the dynamic rendering of tasks adaptive to the users' knowledge.
Why Antiwork: A RoBERTa-Based System forWork-Related Stress Identification and Leading Factor Analysis
Tao Lu, Muzhe Wu, Xinyi Lu, Siyuan Xu, Shuyu Zhan, Anuj Tambwekar, Emily Provost
Abstract Paper
Harsh working environments and work-related stress have been known to contribute to mental health problems such as anxiety, depression, and suicidal ideation. As such, it is paramount to create solutions that can both detect employee unhappiness and find the root cause of the problem. While prior works have examined causes of mental health using machine learning, they typically focus on general mental health analysis, with few of them focusing on explainable solutions or looking at the workplace-specific setting. r/antiwork is a subreddit for the antiwork movement, which is the desire to stop working altogether. Using this subreddit as a proxy for work environment dissatisfaction, we create a new dataset for antiwork sentiment detection and subsequently train a model that highlights the words with antiwork sentiments. Following this, we performed a qualitative and quantitative analysis to uncover some of the key insights into the mindset of individuals who identify with the antiwork movement and how their working environments influenced them. We find that working environments that do not give employees authority or responsibility, frustrating recruiting experiences, and unfair compensation, are some of the leading causes of the antiwork sentiment, resulting in a lack of self-confidence and motivation among their employees.
