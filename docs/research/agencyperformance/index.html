Performance as Agency? Investigating the Trade-off between Sense of Agency and Performance in Target Selection with Preemptive Assistance in VR

Muzhe Wu, Byungjoo Lee, David Lindlbauer

In Submission (IEEE TVCG)

Virtual Reality (VR) systems enable immersive experiences while providing users with efficient interaction techniques for tasks such as selection or manipulation. With the increased integration of AI-powered intelligent assistants, these interaction techniques continue to improve users’ performance by anticipating their goals and actions.

However, it is yet unclear if such advanced interaction techniques negatively influence users’ sense of agency. This research explores this trade-off through a user study on target selection tasks, varying levels of assistance and task difficulty. The results reveal how assistance affects both performance and the sense of agency, offering guidelines for future intelligent interaction techniques.
