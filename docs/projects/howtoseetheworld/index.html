How Do You See The World: Visual Impairment Simulations in VR

CMU 05-899 Interactive Extended Reality Course Project (2023)

My Role: Design & Implementation of the color blindness interactive scenes

Overview

People with normal vision often lack an understanding of the daily challenges faced by individuals with visual impairments. This gap in understanding perpetuates barriers to empathy, inclusivity, and accessible design. To address this, this project immerses participants in a hands-on experience that simulates these challenges through virtual environments, interactive tasks, and reflective debriefings, promoting empathy and driving more inclusive practices in technology and beyond.

Simulated Environments & Tasks

  • Living Room (Cataracts)
    • Activities: Using VR controllers to operate a TV, lamp, and other everyday objects.
    • Focus: Replicating the blurry or cloudy vision commonly associated with cataracts. Participants attempt to read magazines, books, and coffee mugs, highlighting the difficulty of text-based tasks.
  • Kitchen (Color Blindness)
    • Activities: Identifying ripe fruits, sorting them onto color-coded plates, and cooking a steak while watching for signs of burning.
    • Focus: Emphasizing the challenges of distinguishing hues in daily cooking and meal prep, from assessing food freshness to preventing overcooking.
  • Basement (Glaucoma)
    • Activities: Counting and comparing objects on a shelf from a distance.
    • Focus: Demonstrating how reduced peripheral vision—akin to “tunnel vision”—can complicate everyday tasks like locating items on shelves or assessing spatial arrangements.
  • Debrief Museum Scenes: Participants explore panels detailing the causes, treatments, and testing methods for each condition. This session clarifies the nuances and real-world implications that participants may have been missed during the simulations, thus helping them consolidate their in-simulation experiences into a deeper, more empathetic understanding of each impairment.

The implementation was based on post-processing effects in Unity. Cataracts were simulated using depth-of-field effects, color blindness by altering color channels, and glaucoma through a vignette overlay that narrows peripheral vision.
