Why Antiwork: Work-Related Stress Identification and Leading Factor Analysis

UMich EECS448 Human-Centered ML Course Project (2023)

My Role: Data preprocessing, Modeling training, Linguistic Analysis

Overview

Toxic workplaces can contribute to mental health problems like anxiety, depression, and even suicidal ideation. Some of the reasons employees may develop negative feelings towards work are unpleasant working environments, unreasonable workloads, and harsh supervisors. These feelings are referred to as “antiwork sentiment” and can result in harmful negative emotions. This project examines the characteristics and underlying causes of antiwork sentiment and explores methods for predicting such sentiment. The goal is to provide insights into reducing workplace toxicity and enabling timely interventions.

Dataset Collection

We collected data from Reddit, a popular online forum, to analyze the behavioral characteristics of antiwork sentiment. Five subreddits were chosen for their relevance to workplace context: “r/antiwork”, “r/recruiting”, “r/recruitinghell”, “r/work”, and “r/jobs”

  • Size: 329,830 posts and 400,000 comments spanning from 03/27/2009 to 12/31/2022.
  • User Classification: Users are labeled as “antiwork” or “neutral” based on their posting history across these subreddits.

Sentiment Prediction

We explored the following models for antiwork sentiment prediction:

  • SVM: A linear model was used for simple classification tasks with features like word frequency (TF-IDF), linguistic patterns (parts of speech, punctuation), and social engagement (scores, comments, etc.).
  • BERT: A transformer-based encoder that transforms text into feature vectors.
  • RoBERTa-RNN: A robustly optimized BERT model, RoBERTa, was used to encode the text. An RNN was then used to capture dependencies between posts.

Feature Analysis

  • Linguisitic Analysis: LIWC (Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count) was used to analyze linguistic patterns in posts.
  • Topic Modeling: Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was used to identify prominent topics within antiwork posts.

Key Findings

  • Model Performance: The RoBERTa-RNN model achieved 80% accuracy in identifying users with antiwork sentiments.
  • Antiwork Characteristics: Individuals expressing antiwork sentiments exhibit:
    • A lack of self-affirmation and confidence.
    • A decreased focus on personal well-being and daily life activities.
  • Leading Factors for Antiwork:
    • Harsh Working Environments: Lack of respect, feeling unvalued or powerless.
    • Frustrating Recruiting Experiences: Lengthy processes, rejections, and unmet expectations.
    • Unfair Compensation: Concerns about low wages.
